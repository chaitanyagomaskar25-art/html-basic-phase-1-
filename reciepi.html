Delicious Samosa Recipe

A classic Indian snack loved by all

Introduction

  • Samosa is a crispy, golden-fried pastry filled with spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes other vegetables or meat.
  • Popular snack across India and South Asia, often served with chutney or tea.
  • Perfect for parties, tea time, or street-food cravings.

Ingredients

For the Dough:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 4 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water (as needed for kneading)
    • For the Filling:
  • 3 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • Salt to taste
  • 1-2 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
    • For Frying:
  • Oil for deep frying

    • Instructions / Method

    1. Prepare the dough: Mix flour, salt, and oil. Add water gradually and knead into a smooth, firm dough. Cover and rest for 20–30 minutes.
    2. Prepare the filling: Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, chopped green chilies, and ginger. Sauté for a minute.
    3. Add mashed potatoes, peas, and all spices. Mix well. Cook for 5-7 minutes. Add coriander leaves and let it cool.
    4. Shape the samosas: Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each into an oval or circle. Cut it in half. Form a cone from each half and fill with the potato mixture. Seal the edges with water.
    5. Fry the samosas: Heat oil in a pan. Deep-fry samosas on medium heat until golden brown and crispy. Drain excess oil on paper towels.
    6. Serve: Enjoy hot with tamarind or mint chutney.

    Tips

  • Keep the oil medium-hot; too hot and the samosas burn outside, too low and they absorb too much oil.
  • Use firm potatoes to prevent the filling from becoming mushy.
  • You can also make baked samosas for a healthier option.

    • Fun Facts / Extras

  • Samosa is believed to have originated in Central Asia and spread to India in medieval times.
  • There are many variations: meat samosas, sweet samosas, or even chocolate-filled ones for dessert!
  • Pair with chai or any dipping sauce for the ultimate snack experience.
